## 🔍 Overview

<a href="#">ScreenFlow</a> is the professional standard for Mac screen recording and screencasting — combining high-quality capture with a capable timeline editing environment that produces polished tutorial, software demonstration, and presentation videos in a single integrated tool.

The <a href="#">screen capture engine</a> records Mac display output at full resolution with system audio, microphone audio, and camera capture simultaneously — all sources synchronized from the first frame without requiring post-capture sync adjustment. Retina display recording captures at 2x resolution for sharp output when viewed on high-DPI displays. <a href="#">Multiple display recording</a> captures all connected displays simultaneously in a single recording session, with each display available as a separate track in the timeline for selecting which display content to show at each point in the production.

<p align="center">
  <img src="https://www.telestream.net/screenflow/images/ScreenFlow-Laptop.png" alt="ScreenFlow screenshot"/>
</p>

<a href="#">Timeline editing environment</a> organizes the multi-source recording into a standard video editing timeline where screen recording, camera footage, audio, and annotations appear as editable tracks. Trim, cut, rearrange, and add transitions between segments of the recording without leaving ScreenFlow. <a href="#">Zoom and pan effects</a> direct viewer attention to specific screen areas — zooming in on the mouse position during a specific action, panning across a wide interface to show different areas — for tutorial clarity without requiring re-recording with a smaller capture region.

The <a href="#">callout and annotation tools</a> add arrows, text labels, highlight boxes, and freehand drawings on top of the screen recording for visual emphasis during complex interface explanations. <a href="#">Mouse effects</a> highlight the cursor position, amplify click sounds, show keyboard shortcuts as overlay text, and animate right-click actions for viewer awareness of interface interactions that might otherwise be missed.

## 💬 FAQ

<details>
<summary>Does ScreenFlow capture Retina display resolution?</summary>

Yes. ScreenFlow records at the native Retina resolution for 2x pixel density output. Playback on Retina displays maintains sharpness without upscaling artifacts.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can ScreenFlow capture multiple displays simultaneously?</summary>

Yes. All connected displays capture simultaneously in a single session with each display available as an independent track in the timeline.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does ScreenFlow include video editing tools?</summary>

Yes. ScreenFlow includes a timeline editor for trimming, cutting, and arranging the recording with transitions, text overlays, and audio mixing. It is a combined recording and editing environment rather than just a recorder.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can ScreenFlow publish directly to YouTube?</summary>

Yes. Direct export to YouTube, Vimeo, and other video platforms is available through configured platform accounts without requiring manual file download and upload.

</details>
